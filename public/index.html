Anime

Too long didn't read? Check out my Anilist profile instead. I (try) to log everytime I finish my anime and manga. But, too lazy to click the link...? Enjoy the visuals I have provided below.

Below are my personal favorite animes. The list changes every now and then, it depends on which anime resonates me the most. These animes touched me in a personal scale, I rate them the highest out of all.

Below are, still my lovely animes, not as high as my lovechilds, but still I regard them in a special place. I've broken them into genres and demographic just for ease of access.

Mahou Shojou

Magical girls has been something special, atleast recently. I wasn't convinced on why this genre sells. Before the last two years, the only magical girl I've seen was Madoka and a sprinkle of Sailor Moon. Having knee deep in this genre, I absolutely love and adore Magical girls so much. They are just so magical and lovely I just can't help liking them so much. I especially adore the Precure series, as they are very consistent in quality but also just lively at the same time.

Shojou

Ah, Shoujou. If I could watch it all over again. You need to understand that yes, majority of Shojou are Romance, but the way they are written and drawn are far different than the average romance out there. If you are a guy, you might feel less inclined to try out Shoujou, at it obviously appeals women. But let me tell you right now, you are missing out of a gold mine, because shoujou's, they are (in my personal opinion) have a much, richer romance. I urge you to try. I'll...uh...treat you a meal if you don't like my recomendation.

Josei

Josei is a surpringly more niche than Shojou, in terms of anime. And I thought Shoujou had it bad. Josei sells more on manga, so it makes sense. Regardless, it's still a very fun genre. Definitely more mature though, more heavy topics and whatnot. I'd still recommend it!

Shonen

There was a time in my life where I was really hyped for shonen. I devourered alot of mainstream titles, and admiteddly I did have fun. And it's not a bad idea per se, to watch this genre, even though you can't really set your expectations. More than anything, shonen actually helped me more as a social subject to others more than my personal enjoyment. Oh yeah, the memes, slander, and community are pretty fun too. But mainly I feel like an unc towards shonen, I don't really share the same passion. Still fun though!

And no, I haven't watched One Piece. I'm not sure, I'm not particularly interested in it. But, the live action is seriously cool, so let's see if I can stomach 1000 chapters of manga.

Action

It's kinda hard to group of all action as one genre, mostly because different anime utilizes action in a different way. In a sense, Action is usually not the central focus, mostly because sakuga is really expensive to make. But all things considered, I do love a well-animated fight scene, especially choreographed ones that stuns my eyes everytime I see it.

Inspirational

I would categorize them as something out of this world. Eye-shattering visuals? Engaging with political, extreme Ideas? These anime pushes the genre to the limit, and I'm eternally grateful. A single rewatch and I get bountiful of ideas. I'm glad these animes were made. Definitely don't recommend some of these to people because it might be too much, but if you have been into anime for quite some time and is bored of your selection, give these a try. I promise you'd be urged by yourself to make something after watching them. You just can't help but be motivated.

Comfort

I'm someone that fundamentally believes that you should be critical when watching these shows. But being critical is exhausting. Sometimes, you want to just sit back and enjoy some animation without actually needing to think about it. I think this was one of the foundations of the CGDCT Genre? (Cute Girls Doing Cute Things). Heard it's a dangerous pipeline though. But for me, these shows provided me with either laughter of comfort, much so whenever I watch them it triggers a theurapatic response. Just a nice time watching these shows, man.

Romance

There was a time in my life when I was a very, very hopeless romantic. I've moved one, but I've read and watched so many romance animes that I no longer yearn? I still do, but all those wanting to date angst has been fully patched by Shoujou and Romance. I like to think back and remind myself how silly it is for me t watch a romance show and everytime a cute moment passes I prayed to god that could happen to me. And It did! Some of it, atleast. Still working on it. I find romance animes are the best when you're actulaly in a relationship and the little stuff reminds you of those Romance animes. Ah, good old days.
